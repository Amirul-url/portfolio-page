Underwater images suffer visual quality degradation from light absorption and scattering, resulting in low contrast, poor sharpness, and greenish-bluish dominance that impedes effective marine analysis. This study develops a hybrid enhancement system integrating Dark Channel  Prior (DCP), CNN U-Net-based Image Formation Model (FI), and White Balance (WB), trained on UIEB, LSUI, and EUVP datasets with
128x128 patch preprocessing and data augmentation. Performance is evaluated via all metrics with PCQI improved by 18-28%, UIQM improved by 25-40%, SSIM improved by 22-35%, entropy improved by 15-30%, and UCIQE improved by 20-32% on 30 test images representing haze, low contrast, and colour distortion, where the fusion method achieves significant improvements over originals and single techniques (Tables 5.5-5.13). The PyQt5 GUI-enabled system demonstrates automated restoration of visibility, contrast, sharpness, and colour, supporting real-world AUV monitoring and underwater exploration applications.
